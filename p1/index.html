About Bangladesh

Bangladesh, also known as the Land of the Bengal, was once a part of British India until the nation gained independence in 1947. It broke off from Pakistan in 1971 in a long and bloody confilct and is now one of the fastest growing economies in the developing world. Despite its constant evolution the nation retains its own culture and the people have their own distinct lifestyle. From the crowded megacities to the rural areas, this nation is a sight to behold.

Things to do in Bangladesh

In a country with such rich heritage, there are a multitude of things to do there. With a sense of curiosity and your credible it is possible foreigners and tourists to try all of these activities.

  • Take a tour of Old Dhaka
  • Visit the Lakkatura Tea Garden
  • Dare to venture into the Sundarban Jungle

Theres a great amount of flora and fauna in this land of the Bengal

Just about every harmless crickets to scaly reptile exists somewhere in the countryside. The Bengla Tiger remains the apex predator in the heavily dense sundurban region. According to the World Wildlife Fund, these tigers are officially an endangered species. Because of that, it it is illegal to try to find or hunt them by yourself - not that anyone would try to face a Bengal Tiger by themselves.
